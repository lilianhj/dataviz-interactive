Refugee Flow into the United States: Variation Across Time and Space

Where Do We Come From? What Are We? Where Are We Going?

The visualizations on this website display how the flow of refugees into the United States varies across time and space.

Variation across time

Every year, the President sets the overall "ceiling" - a maximum number of refugees that can be admitted to the United States - and allocates that number among different regions. The bar chart below shows how many refugees are actually admitted from each region in a year, and how this differs from the refugee ceiling - whether it exceeds or falls short of it.

You can select any range of years to view, from 2001 to 2019, by dragging the handles of the slider below.

Distribution across space

Once these refugees have been admitted to the United States, where do they go? We shift our attention from the origins of refugees, to take a more granular look at their destinations. The map below shows the placement of refugees in different states within the United States in 2018.

You can click on a state to explore the exact breakdown of which refugees were placed in that state.
